Early Life

Jon Kortajarena Redruello was born on May 19, 1985, in Bilbao, in Spain's Basque Country. He attended an ikastola school until age ten, then moved between public, private, and Catholic schools due to family travel. He speaks Basque, Spanish, and English.

Discovery and Modeling Career

In September 2003, at age 18, Kortajarena was vacationing in Barcelona when a booker spotted him at a fashion show he was attending with a friend. His first professional booking was a Roberto Verino show at Madrid Fashion Week, after which he moved to Paris to pursue modeling full time. In 2004, he became the face of Roberto Cavalli's Just Cavalli spring campaign, shot by Ellen von Unwerth, and shot a Versace campaign photographed by Steven Meisel the same year.

Terry Richardson photographed Kortajarena's first Tom Ford campaign in 2007, beginning a partnership in which he served as the designer's signature male model for nine consecutive seasons — by 2009, Tom Ford was directing and photographing him personally. In 2008, L'Officiel Hommes devoted its entire spring/summer issue, 200 pages, to Kortajarena, the first time a male model had been given such treatment by the magazine. He went on to model for Karl Lagerfeld, Salvatore Ferragamo, Valentino, Giorgio Armani, Chanel, Hugo Boss, Guerlain, Bally, Etro, Trussardi, Diesel, Bulgari, H&M, Zara, and Guess, and fronted Lacoste's $66 million "Unconventional Chic" campaign in 2011, shot by Mert and Marcus.

Forbes & Vogue

On June 26, 2009, Forbes ranked Kortajarena eighth on its list of "The World's 10 Most Successful Male Models." Vogue US has described him as one of the most important male models of all time, and he won "Model of the Year" from German GQ and "Best Spanish Model" from Marie Claire Spain in 2009.

Acting Career

Kortajarena made his acting debut in Tom Ford's directorial debut A Single Man (2009), alongside Colin Firth and Julianne Moore. He went on to appear in Penélope Cruz's short film L'Agent Provocateur and the film Ma Ma, the Spanish film Acantilado, the science-fiction film Andron alongside Alec Baldwin, a recurring role in the American series Quantico, the Netflix series High Seas, the Movistar+ series El Inmortal, an episode of Tales from the Loop, and the Netflix series Machos Alfa.

Personal Life

Kortajarena has served as an ambassador for Save the Children and Greenpeace, and created "Kortajarena Forest," a reforestation project near Madrid. He was in a relationship with Welsh actor Luke Evans from 2014 to 2016.
