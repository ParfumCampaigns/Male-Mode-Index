Early Life

Brad Kroenig was born on April 23, 1979, in the St. Louis suburb of Oakville, Missouri, to an environmental engineer father and a legal assistant mother. He excelled in soccer and basketball at Oakville High School, graduating in 1998, and went on to Florida International University on a soccer scholarship, with plans to pursue the sport professionally.

Discovery and Early Career

At around age 20, a friend encouraged Kroenig to try modeling. After being turned down by two Miami Beach agencies, a third accepted him, and he modeled in Miami for six months before moving to Los Angeles and then New York, where he signed a three-year contract with Ford Models in 2001 — an agency relationship that has lasted more than two decades. He left college to pursue modeling full time. His first major booking was a nude Abercrombie & Fitch shoot, and in 2001 he landed a Ralph Lauren campaign that helped launch his career; in 2002, he worked alongside Naomi Campbell and appeared on Abercrombie's shopping bags. In 2003, he made his runway debut for Jil Sander and Dolce & Gabbana.

Karl Lagerfeld and Chanel

Kroenig met Karl Lagerfeld in 2003 during a VMAN magazine photo shoot at Lagerfeld's villa in Biarritz, France; Lagerfeld reportedly took some 20 photographs of Kroenig that day, far more than of any other model present. The encounter began a close professional and personal relationship that lasted until Lagerfeld's death in 2019. Kroenig became one of "Karl's Boys," accompanying the designer on business trips and holidays — including annual stretches in St. Tropez — and was sometimes mistakenly identified in the press as Lagerfeld's boyfriend. In 2007, Lagerfeld published Metamorphoses of an American, a photography book devoted entirely to Kroenig.

Kroenig became a fixture of Chanel's runway and advertising under Lagerfeld's direction, and also modeled for Fendi, Ralph Lauren, Dolce & Gabbana, Gap, Hermès, Roberto Cavalli, DKNY, and Adidas. In 2015, the New York Times Magazine profiled him under the headline "The Jet Set Life of Karl Lagerfeld's Favorite Male Model — for Now."

Vogue

Vogue named Kroenig one of the "Top 10 Male Models of All Time." He appeared on the covers of Vogue, Allure, VMAN, and Numéro Homme over the course of his career.

Personal Life and Later Career

Kroenig is married to Nicole Kroenig, daughter of tennis coach Nick Bollettieri; the couple met in Sarasota, Florida, in 2007. They have two sons, Hudson (born 2008) and Jameson (born 2011). Hudson is Karl Lagerfeld's godson and began modeling for Chanel at age two, going on to appear regularly on the brand's runway. After more than two decades in fashion, Kroenig and his wife earned real estate licenses and now run a residential real estate practice with Coldwell Banker on Longboat Key and in Lakewood Ranch, Florida, while Kroenig has continued modeling on a reduced basis for select international brands.
