Early Life

Werner Schreyer was born on March 10, 1970, in Vienna, Austria. He graduated from the European School of Economic and Social Sciences in Vienna. Without a clear career direction afterward, he was encouraged by his mother to try modeling, since, as he has said, the clothes he wore always seemed to fit him as if tailor-made. He joined an Austrian modeling agency and quickly began working internationally.

Discovery and Modeling Career

Schreyer has modeled since 1990, working with photographers including Herb Ritts, Bruce Weber, Mario Testino, Richard Avedon, and Juergen Teller, for brands including Versace, Giorgio Armani, Guess, Calvin Klein, Gucci, Dolce & Gabbana, Prada, Hugo Boss, Ralph Lauren, Burberry, and Givenchy. One of his most recognized images is his 1992 Levi's campaign, shot by Herb Ritts; he has also cited his 2011 Louis Vuitton menswear campaign under creative director Kim Jones, photographed by Alasdair McLellan, among his career highlights. He appeared on the covers of Elle and GQ, and became the only male model to appear on the cover of French Vogue.

Industry Standing

Schreyer has been described by his agency as one of only five male models considered supermodels and legends of the industry, having modeled and appeared in films worldwide since the 1980s. His resemblance to James Dean earned him the nickname "the James Dean of Fashion."

Acting Career

Schreyer trained as a method actor at the Gérard Vergez school in Paris and as a stuntman at Action Concept in Cologne. His acting debut came in the 1993 costume drama Senso (referred to in some English-language coverage as Sense), which won first prize at the International Historic Film Festival. He went on to appear in Élisa alongside Gérard Depardieu and Vanessa Paradis, and worked on a project with Mickey Rourke. He was at one point considered for the role of designer Wolfgang Joop in a planned biographical film about Joop, though the project did not proceed with him in the role.

Later Career and Personal Life

In the 2000s, Schreyer turned toward the visual arts, completing studies at Zurich's F+F School of Art and Media Design in 2010 and presenting his first exhibition, "L'autre moi," in Vienna in 2012. He now lives near Zurich, Switzerland, and continues to return to Austria for events. He has described his interests as sports, soccer, martial arts, mountain biking, running, and nature.
