Early Life

Anthony Borden Ward was born on June 10, 1963, in Santa Cruz, California, to Robert Borden Ward and Karen Elizabeth Castro. He is of English, Scottish, Irish, Swedish, and Portuguese descent.

Ward spent his childhood in San Jose before moving to Sonora, California, where he graduated from high school. He subsequently relocated to Los Angeles to pursue a career in modeling, acting, and dance. He was discovered by a scout while attending West Valley College in Saratoga, California, and began modeling at age 18.

Modeling Career

Ward launched his modeling career in 1981 with Calvin Klein underwear, shot by Herb Ritts — one of the most recognizable campaigns in the history of American fashion advertising. That debut established Ward as a face of singular intensity: unconventional in a decade that rewarded exactly that quality.

Over a career spanning four decades, Ward worked with Karl Lagerfeld, Steven Klein, Steven Meisel, and Terry Richardson. His campaigns include Roberto Cavalli, Chanel, Dolce & Gabbana, Diesel, Fendi, H&M, and Hugo Boss. He has been signed simultaneously to DNA Model Management in New York, Why Not Model Agency in Milan, Premier Model Management in London, Photogenics in Los Angeles, Unique Models in Copenhagen, and Bananas Models in Paris — a roster reflecting the sustained international demand for his work across six major markets.

Ward also established his own fashion brand, SixInTheFace — "Hand Ravaged Clothing by Mr. Ward" — and has worked as a painter and photographer throughout his career, adding a creative dimension rarely seen among models of his generation.

Music Videos and Cultural Impact

Tony Ward is one of the few models in history whose face became inseparable from the cultural identity of a major pop artist. Beginning in 1989, he appeared in three of Madonna's most iconic music videos: Cherish (1989), Justify My Love (1990), and Erotica (1992). He also appeared in Madonna's controversial Sex book (1992) and was her partner in the early 1990s.

Beyond Madonna, Ward appeared in George Michael's Fastlove (1996) and the Spice Girls' Say You'll Be There (1996) — two of the defining pop videos of the decade. His music video work across three of the era's biggest global acts is without parallel in male modeling history.

Acting Career

Ward's acting career began in earnest in 1996 with a starring role in Hustler White, directed by Bruce LaBruce and Rick Castro. He subsequently appeared in Sex/Life in L.A. (1998), Out in Fifty (1999), All About the Benjamins (2002), Story of Jen (2007, lead role), and L.A. Zombie (2010). His most recent screen credit is All Man: International Male (2022).

Legacy

Tony Ward's career defies easy categorization. Across four decades he has worked as a model, actor, painter, photographer, and fashion designer — rarely confining himself to a single discipline and never allowing his image to be owned entirely by any one brand or era.

His Calvin Klein debut with Herb Ritts in 1981 remains one of the foundational images in male fashion advertising. His music video work with Madonna, George Michael, and the Spice Girls brought his face to audiences far beyond the fashion world. His longevity — still active more than forty years after his first campaign — places him among a handful of male models whose careers cannot be measured by a single peak moment.

Ward is represented simultaneously across six international agencies and continues to work.
