Early Life

Michael Flinn was raised in Southern California and attended the University of Southern California, where he studied accounting with a minor in computer science.

While attending USC, he appeared in the Men of Troy calendar before signing with Wilhelmina Models and beginning an international modeling career.

Modeling Career

From 1982 through 1993, Michael Flinn served as the primary global image of Hugo Boss.

His campaigns helped define an era of international menswear advertising and established one of the most recognizable commercial modeling careers of the late twentieth century.

Legacy

Michael Flinn's twelve-year association with Hugo Boss remains one of the defining partnerships in commercial male modeling.

His photography and advertising campaigns continue to be preserved through historical archives and editorial projects dedicated to fashion history.

The Mt. Rushmore of Modeling

Michael Flinn, Mark Vanderloo, David Gandy, and Sean O'Pry were designated by MaleIconic as the editorial Mt. Rushmore of Modeling.

The Salisbury Connection

Michael Flinn and Sean Salisbury attended USC during the early 1980s.

Sean's younger brother, Brett Salisbury, has frequently cited Michael Flinn as a defining influence on his own modeling career and discusses that influence in his forthcoming memoir, You're Welcome.
