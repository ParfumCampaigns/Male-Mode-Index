Early Life

Alton Devon Mason was born on November 21, 1997, in Nebraska, to a family of Jamaican, Ghanaian, and Haitian heritage. His father, Alton Mason Sr., is a former professional basketball player, and his mother, Anita Mason, is a former model from Gilbert, Arizona. He is the eldest of four children. Due to his father's overseas basketball career, the family lived in eleven European countries — including Greece, Belgium, Bosnia, and the Netherlands — over 13 years before settling in Phoenix, Arizona. At 17, he moved to Los Angeles to study dance and acting at the American Musical and Dramatic Academy.

Discovery and Modeling Career

Through his godmother, choreographer Laurieann Gibson, Mason landed a job as a backup dancer for Diddy at the 2015 BET Awards. He was subsequently discovered on Instagram by scouts and cast in Kanye West's Yeezy Season 3 show at Madison Square Garden in February 2016, his first major modeling booking, and signed with IMG Models. He went on to walk for Gucci's Fall 2017 show in Milan, and by 2018 and 2019 was appearing for Versace, Louis Vuitton — where he performed impromptu backflips down Virgil Abloh's runway — and Valentino.

On December 4, 2018, Mason became the first Black male model to walk for Chanel, at the house's Métiers d'Art Paris–New York show under Karl Lagerfeld. He has gone on to model for Puma, Fenty x Puma, Michael Kors, Philipp Plein, Jeremy Scott, Tom Ford, Fendi, Karl Lagerfeld, Off-White, Rabanne, Dolce & Gabbana, Balenciaga, Zara, H&M, Lacoste, and BMW, and appeared in a Chanel short film wearing designs by Pharrell Williams. He has cited Naomi Campbell as his biggest modeling inspiration.

Industry Honors

GQ Australia named Mason Male Model of the Year in 2019, and Models.com gave him the same title for five consecutive years. Forbes included him in its "30 Under 30" list for Art & Style in 2021. As of September 2023, he has been reported as the highest-paid male supermodel in the world, and GQ has referred to him as "the world's only male supermodel."

Acting and Music

Mason portrayed Little Richard in Baz Luhrmann's 2022 biopic Elvis, and has since appeared in the films Dream Scenario and Van Gogh. In 2020, he released his debut single, "Gimme Gimme," filming its music video in Lagos, Nigeria, as part of the soundtrack to the short film Rise in Light, in which he also starred; the project raised more than $10,000 for the Aga Khan Foundation to support Nigerian communities.

Personal Life

Mason has been in a relationship with fellow model Anok Yai, the first South Sudanese model and second Black model after Naomi Campbell to open a Prada show. He supports organizations including the Trevor Project and the NAACP.
