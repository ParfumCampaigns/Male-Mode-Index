Early Life

Michael John Bergin was born on March 18, 1969, in Naugatuck, Connecticut. He attended the University of Connecticut, where he studied business marketing, and began modeling almost by accident: a friend kept pushing him to try it during his sophomore year, and he initially agreed mainly because modeling paid well enough to help cover his tuition.

As the work grew and the commute to New York became more frequent, Bergin spent the summer after his junior year working in Manhattan full time. He nonetheless returned to UConn to finish his degree before moving back to New York permanently to model full time.

Modeling Career

Bergin's breakthrough came when Calvin Klein selected him, then a relatively unknown face, to replace Mark Wahlberg in the brand's black-and-white underwear campaign. He appeared both solo and alongside Kate Moss in Calvin Klein's jeans, underwear, and suit advertising, including a large-format billboard in Times Square, and the campaign made him one of the defining male faces of 1990s advertising.

He went on to walk runways in New York, Paris, and Milan for Sonia Rykiel, Valentino, Calvin Klein, Gianfranco Ferré, Giorgio Armani, Yves Saint Laurent, and Donna Karan, and was the exclusive model for Claiborne for Men, a division of Liz Claiborne, for more than three years. His commercial and print work included campaigns for Kellogg's, Bacardi Rum, L'Oréal, Maybelline, Coty, Valentino, and Perry Ellis. He was represented by the Click agency early in his career and later by Wilhelmina Models and New York Model Management.

Editorial Rankings and Recognition

L'Uomo Vogue · Top 25 Male Models Ever

Michael Bergin appears on the L'Uomo Vogue Top 25 Male Models Ever list, as reported by SELF Magazine via Yahoo! Shine on August 22, 2012. The entry highlights his UConn background and his run as Calvin Klein's successor to Mark Wahlberg, and notes that the Times Square billboard bearing his image became one of the era's best-known pieces of fashion advertising.

Acting Career

Bergin's modeling fame led to television and film work. He appeared in two 1995 episodes of the CBS drama Central Park West, had a notable cameo in Ismail Merchant's 1996 film The Proprietor, and guest-starred on the CBS sitcom Cosby in 1998.

He is best known for playing lifeguard Jack "J.D." Darius on Baywatch from 1997 to 1999 and its continuation, Baywatch Hawaii, from 1999 to 2001 — four seasons in total. In 2000 he appeared in the gay-themed ensemble comedy The Broken Hearts Club: A Romantic Comedy, and in 2005 he co-starred with former Baywatch castmate Erika Eleniak in the film Fatal Reunion. In 2026, it was announced that Bergin would reprise the role of J.D. Darius in a recurring capacity for a rebooted Baywatch series, and he also appeared in the 2024 Hulu docuseries After Baywatch: Moment in the Sun.

Carolyn Bessette and The Other Man

In the early 1990s, while she was working in public relations for Calvin Klein, Bergin began dating Carolyn Bessette. Their relationship continued for a couple of years even as Bessette grew close to John F. Kennedy Jr., whom she married in September 1996. Kennedy and Bessette-Kennedy died in a plane crash in July 1999.

In 2004, Bergin published The Other Man: John F. Kennedy Jr., Carolyn Bessette, and Me, in which he wrote that he and Bessette had continued seeing each other during her marriage to Kennedy. Bergin has said publicly that he believes she still cared for him after her marriage. Both the relationship and the book have since been portrayed in television treatments of the Kennedy-Bessette story, including a 2026 dramatization of their romance.

Personal Life

Bergin married makeup artist Joy Tilk on September 24, 2004; they have two children, Jesse (born 2000) and Alana (born 2004). His son Jesse was drafted by the Miami Marlins in the 11th round of the 2021 MLB Draft. Bergin began a second career in real estate in Beverly Hills in 2001. He has served as a national spokesperson for the anti-drug program D.A.R.E. and has supported numerous AIDS charities.
