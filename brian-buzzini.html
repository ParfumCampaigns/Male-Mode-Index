Early Life

Brian Buzzini was born on August 18, 1962, in Modesto, California, to a father who had been a professional golfer and a mother who had been a professional tennis player. He grew up describing himself as a "country boy" from California's Central Valley farmland, and grew to a build of roughly 6'1½"–6'2" with a broad-shouldered, athletic frame.

Discovery and Modeling Career

Buzzini was discovered around 1983 by Gary Loftus, an agent and owner of Elite Model Management's San Francisco office, while Loftus was scouting roughly 90 miles east of the city in Modesto. Loftus initially pushed Buzzini toward a leaner, more conventional runway build, which Buzzini resisted, opting instead to lean into his athletic frame. Photographer Martin Ryter, who worked alongside Loftus, began shooting Buzzini regularly in his early test years.

Buzzini began working with the International Male catalog in 1984, and over the following decade became one of its most recognizable faces, developing what is often described as one of the largest gay followings of any male model of his era. His highest-profile exposure came through Playgirl magazine: in June 1985, at age 22, he appeared nude in the two-page centerfold of the magazine's twelfth-anniversary issue, and in January 1986, at 23, he returned for a photospread that named him Playgirl's "Man of the Year." He is frequently credited — "by most accounts," per one profile — as one of the first male models to achieve supermodel status.

Through the mid-1980s and 1990s, Buzzini modeled for Calvin Klein, Versace, and Perry Ellis, and became the original "Cool Water Man" in Davidoff's Cool Water fragrance campaign, fronting its television commercials in the early-to-mid 1990s. He also appeared in advertising for Diet Coke, Kool cigarettes, Burberry, Edge shaving cream, and a 1987–88 Bud Light campaign, and was photographed by Herb Ritts, Bruce Weber, Victor Skrebneski, and Albert Watson.

Recognition

THE 100 / MaleIconic

Buzzini is featured in THE 100: The Definitive Record of the Greatest Male Models in History and profiled on MaleIconic.com, which describes itself as an editorial companion built from the Male Model Index's rankings.

Male Model Index

Buzzini is ranked No. 92 in Tier 5 ("Verified Major Campaign Participants") of the Male Model Index, a private ranking registry, for his Davidoff Cool Water fragrance work and lifestyle print campaigns.

Male Model Index

Editorial note: MaleIconic and the Male Model Index are run by the same editor (James Conrad) and describe themselves as companion projects rather than independent sources — MaleIconic's own "About" page states it built its list starting from the Male Model Index's rankings.

The Commercial Male — The Consensus

Buzzini appears at No. 97 in "The Consensus," a composite ranking site thecommercialmale.com compiles across eight separate male-model ranking systems. For Buzzini specifically, only two of those eight systems — the Male Model Index and MaleIconic — include him; he does not appear on the original 1972–2012 L'Uomo Vogue list, "The Greatest 25" tribute list, theFashionSpot's gallery, or the UOMO Modello power rankings. His composite position is therefore a reprocessing of the same two rankings already cited above, not additional independent corroboration.

Acting

Buzzini made occasional forays into acting following his modeling career. He appeared in Gregg Araki's 1997 independent film Nowhere, credited in a supporting role, and in the 2000 short film 12 Rounds, in which he played a bisexual boxer. In 2022, he appeared as himself in the documentary All Man: The International Male Story, discussing the catalog's cultural impact; he has said he watched the film with his adult daughters, who saw his modeling-era work for the first time.

Personal Life

Buzzini has four daughters. In interviews he has discussed launching a fitness program aimed at men over 50 and a men's underwear line, Buzzini Essentials, alongside motorbike racing as a longtime personal interest.
