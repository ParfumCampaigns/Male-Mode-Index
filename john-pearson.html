Early Life

John Pearson was born circa 1966 in Hull (Kingston upon Hull), Yorkshire, England. He grew up in Sheffield and Hull, in the north of England.

At eighteen, while working as a manager of a denim shop in Hull, a photographer walked in and asked to take his picture — an encounter Pearson has described as entirely unexpected, and the moment that redirected his life.

Three years later, in 1986, he relocated to New York, arriving at the precise moment the supermodel era was taking shape. Within days of landing, he had shot the cover of Self magazine alongside Uma Thurman.

Modeling Career

Over the following decades, Pearson became one of the most sought-after male models in the world. His client list included Calvin Klein, Ralph Lauren, Donna Karan, Valentino, Burberry, Levi's, Gap, Banana Republic, and Guy Laroche, and he appeared in more than 100 commercials.

On the editorial side, he worked across Vogue Italia, L'Uomo Vogue, Vogue Hommes, and VMAN, and maintained a continuous presence in American GQ for over thirty years — a record unique among male models.

The photographers he worked with read as a catalogue of the era's greatest image-makers: Herb Ritts, Bruce Weber, Helmut Newton, Peter Lindbergh, Mario Testino, Patrick Demarchelier, Steven Meisel, David Bailey, Arthur Elgort, and Ellen von Unwerth, among others. Of Pearson, Herb Ritts said: "Mr Pearson is a natural who needs no guidance or direction."

Legacy

What makes Pearson's career structurally unusual is its duration. Most male models of the 1990s supermodel era had careers measured in a handful of years. Pearson worked at the top level for more than three decades, maintaining an active commercial client list while continuing to appear in leading editorial publications.

His story — discovered by chance at eighteen in a denim shop in Yorkshire, relocated to New York, and within days shooting magazine covers alongside future icons — is among the origin stories of the male supermodel era itself.

He lives in Los Angeles with his wife Alison Edmond and their three children.

Freedom! '90

Pearson's most visible cultural moment came when he was cast alongside Naomi Campbell, Linda Evangelista, Cindy Crawford, and Christy Turlington in George Michael's Freedom! '90 music video, directed by David Fincher. The video became one of the defining documents of the supermodel era and placed Pearson alongside the most iconic models of the period.

Mr. Feel Good

Beyond the runway and campaign work, Pearson served as a contributing editor at British GQ and as consulting editor for Taschen's Soul i-D, writing the foreword for that volume.

He later co-founded Mr. Feel Good, a lifestyle, culture, and personal development digital magazine aimed at men, with journalist Pete Samson and creative director Alison Edmond. The Rich Roll Podcast, which featured him in 2023, called him "the world's first male supermodel" and noted his focus on healthy masculinity, fatherhood, and finding purpose in aging — themes that now define much of his public work through the magazine.
