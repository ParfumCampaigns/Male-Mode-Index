Early Life

Alex Lundqvist was born on April 14, 1972, in Stockholm, Sweden. He served in the Swedish military as a tank platoon commander, and afterward studied computer design with an eye toward a career in advertising before a girlfriend suggested he try modeling.

Discovery and Modeling Career

In the summer of 1994, Lundqvist was tracked down by photographer Bruce Weber and Wilhelmina agent Sean Patterson after they spotted his photo in an obscure publication. Versace immediately booked him for a worldwide campaign with Helena Christensen, shot by Weber, which led directly to Karl Lagerfeld campaigns in Europe and Guess campaigns in the United States.

In February 1995, Lundqvist walked runway shows in Paris, Milan, and New York for Valentino, Versace, Armani, Gucci, and Calvin Klein. That April, he appeared in a Lagerfeld campaign with Nadja Auermann, and within days was cast in the 1995 Guess campaign alongside Laetitia Casta and Valeria Mazza, followed by further Guess campaigns and television commercials through 1997. He appeared in GQ, Playgirl, Elle, L'Uomo Vogue, Top Model, and Paper Magazine, and was interviewed on MTV's House of Style alongside his close friend and fellow model Mark Vanderloo.

Other Ventures

Beyond modeling, Lundqvist has had a long parallel career as a professional paintball player, competing for Jersey Authority in the National Professional Paintball League and Russian Legion in the PSP National X-Ball League; his brother, Maximus Lundqvist, has also competed professionally in paintball with Stockholm's Joy Division team. Lundqvist appeared in Fergie's 2007 music video "Clumsy," and has since taken on acting roles, including in the films The Limit (2014) and Maki (2018).

Personal Life

Lundqvist's native language is Swedish; he also speaks fluent English and German, along with some Italian and French. He lives in New York City.
