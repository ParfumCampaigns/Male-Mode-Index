Early Life

David James Gandy was born on February 19, 1980, in Billericay, Essex, England, to Brenda Janet Gandy and Christopher A. Gandy, who ran a property and freight company. He studied marketing at the University of Gloucestershire.

Gandy's entry into modeling was accidental: a university flatmate submitted a video of him, without his knowledge, to a model-search competition on the British daytime television program This Morning. He won the competition at age 21, which came with a contract at Select Model Management in London.

Modeling Career

In an era when male modeling favored a slim, waifish build, Gandy deliberately built a more athletic, muscular frame — a choice that pushed back against the trend set by designers like Hedi Slimane at Dior Homme and helped shift the standard for male models that followed.

Early in his career he modeled for Shiatzy Chen, 7 For All Mankind, Zara, Marks & Spencer, Gant USA, Hugo Boss, Russell & Bromley, H&M, Carolina Herrera, and Massimo Dutti. In 2006 he became the face of Dolce & Gabbana, a role that would define his career for over a decade.

The Dolce & Gabbana Years

Gandy's breakout came with the 2007 campaign for Dolce & Gabbana's fragrance "Light Blue," co-starring Marija Vujovic and shot by Mario Testino. The campaign drew 11 million online views and put a 50-foot billboard of Gandy in New York's Times Square. He went on to model for the brand's 2008 calendar, shot by Mariano Vivanco, and in 2011 Dolce & Gabbana published David Gandy by Dolce & Gabbana, a 280-page photographic book chronicling their collaboration. The Light Blue campaign was relaunched in 2013 with Bianca Balti, again shot by Testino, on location in Capri.

By 2012 he was appearing on 16 magazine covers and 18 editorial spreads a year, and continued modeling for brands including Banana Republic, Lucky Brand Jeans, and Marks & Spencer, alongside a run as brand ambassador for Johnnie Walker Blue Label.

Forbes & Industry Honors

Gandy is the only male model to appear on Forbes' list of the world's highest-paid models more than once, and is widely cited as the highest-grossing male model in history. Spanish Glamour named him "Most Beautiful International Male" in 2008. The British Fashion Council nominated him for Model of the Year in both 2010 and 2012 — the first male model to receive the nomination — and he later joined the BFC committee that launched London Collections: Men. British GQ has repeatedly placed him on its list of Best Dressed Men and named him Most Stylish Man of the Year in 2015.

During the closing ceremony of the 2012 London Olympics, Gandy was the only male model to walk the Union Jack-shaped catwalk of British fashion, alongside Kate Moss, Naomi Campbell, and other leading models. He was named to the Evening Standard's "1,000 Most Influential People in London" in both 2012 and 2013.

Business Ventures

In 2014, Gandy launched the David Gandy for Autograph range at Marks & Spencer, covering underwear, sleepwear, swim, and loungewear, which became one of the retailer's top-selling clothing lines. In 2021 he founded David Gandy Wellwear, an apparel label built around fabric performance. He has also released two mobile apps, the David Gandy Men's Style Guide and the David Gandy Fitness Guide, and writes regularly for British Vogue, British GQ, and previously the Telegraph.

Gandy's charity work includes founding the Blue Steel Appeal for Comic Relief's Red Nose Day and serving as the first celebrity ambassador for Battersea Dogs & Cats Home.

Personal Life

Gandy lives in Gloucestershire. He has two daughters, Matilda and Tabitha.

David Gandy's career continues to be documented through independent editorial archives dedicated to preserving the history of commercial male modeling.
