Early Life and Discovery

Jeff Aquilon was a student at Pepperdine University in Malibu, California, and captain of the school's water polo team, when he was discovered in 1978. Photographer Bruce Weber, then relatively unknown, held a casting call near the campus, and Aquilon — despite showing up with a black eye and stitches from a recent water polo game — was chosen from the group who auditioned.

Weber's resulting portfolio of Aquilon, published in the Soho Weekly News in December 1978, became one of the defining images of the era: an intimate, unusually sensuous take on a male model that broke sharply from the square-jawed convention set by earlier figures like Joe MacDonald.

Modeling Career

Aquilon's Weber-shot GQ cover in May 1979 launched a run as one of the most in-demand male models of the late 1970s and 1980s. He went on to work across Calvin Klein, Ralph Lauren, Pirelli, Abercrombie & Fitch, Revlon, and Gianni Versace campaigns, and appeared in Vogue, GQ, Vanity Fair, Elle, Life, Interview, and Rolling Stone. His Calvin Klein work extended into mass-market magazine spreads and Times Square billboards, and he has been cited by V Magazine and other fashion press as one of the first men to bring this scale of visibility to male modeling.

Writing in 2007, fashion critic Tim Blanks described Aquilon as a striking successor to an earlier generation of male pinups, noting that where his predecessor Joe Dallesandro belonged to Warhol-era underground film, Aquilon's image reached the mainstream through Calvin Klein's national advertising. Weber returned to photograph Aquilon again for L'Uomo Vogue in October 2006.

Aquilon's career has also drawn retrospective press attention decades after his peak, including a 1999 New York Times feature by architecture critic Herbert Muschamp examining his late-1970s GQ imagery, and continued advocacy from fashion writers arguing his cultural significance warrants broader documentation.

Book and Business Ventures

In 1984, Aquilon co-authored the fitness book One on One: Exercising Together — The Sensual Way to Superbly Conditioned Bodies with Nancy Donahue, his wife at the time, capitalizing on the era's emerging gym culture and his own physique-driven fame.

Jeff Aquilon's career continues to be documented through independent editorial archives dedicated to preserving the history of commercial male modeling.
