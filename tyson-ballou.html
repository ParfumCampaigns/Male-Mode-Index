Early Life

Tyson Brett Ballou was born on November 14, 1976, in Garland, Texas, the eldest of three sons of Larry and Nancy Ballou. His father died in an accident when Tyson was a young boy. He played basketball for four years at North Garland High School, graduated in 1995, and briefly enrolled at Richland College before modeling took over his schedule.

Discovery and Modeling Career

At 15, Ballou was discovered by Susan Kaleta of Dallas' Page Parkes modeling agency and began working local shows and campaigns in Texas, including for J.C. Penney and Foley's. In 1997, scouts from Ford Models recruited him to New York, and in September 1998 he switched to IMG, committing to live in Manhattan full time — a move that came with a new, closer-cropped look.

Ballou went on to model for Calvin Klein, Versace, Giorgio Armani, Dolce & Gabbana, Tommy Hilfiger, Jil Sander, DKNY, Hugo Boss, Byblos, Moschino, Emporio Armani, Salvatore Ferragamo, Louis Vuitton, Valentino, and Perry Ellis, among others, and appeared in L'Uomo Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, Arena Homme Plus, The Face, Numéro, W, Details, and VMAN. In 2007, he starred in a 7 For All Mankind jeans campaign alongside Carolyn Murphy, and in 2018 Steven Klein photographed him in a 34-page portfolio with two covers for Man About Town's Winter/Spring issue. His career has now spanned more than three decades.

Personal Life

Ballou was in a relationship with Brazilian model Fernanda Tavares from roughly 2003 to 2006. He is an avid basketball fan of the New York Knicks and Dallas Mavericks and maintains a public presence on Instagram, where he shares his interests in food, sports, and music.
