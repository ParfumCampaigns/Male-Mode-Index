Modeling Career

Ric Arango is best known for his work with photographer Bruce Weber, most notably the 1989 silver gelatin series Point of Conception. The photographs, taken in the same period as Weber's celebrated Calvin Klein and Ralph Lauren campaign work, show Arango captured mid-jump from a low camera angle against open sky — images that galleries have since described as near-sculptural studies of the male form, in the visual lineage of photographers like Horst P. Horst and George Hoyningen-Huene.

Arango's image circulated further within the era's Bruce Weber circle alongside other models of the period, including in shared prints and archival material referencing "Deryl George & Ric Arango" sittings from the same era.

A Note on Sourcing

Unlike many of his contemporaries from the same era, detailed biographical information about Ric Arango — early life, agency representation, other campaigns — is not well documented in independently verifiable sources. This entry is limited to what can be confirmed through gallery, archival, and photographic-history documentation of his work with Bruce Weber.
