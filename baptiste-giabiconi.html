Early Life

Baptiste Giabiconi was born on November 9, 1989, in Marignane, in the Bouches-du-Rhône department of France, to a Corsican family — his father from Bastia and his mother, Marie-France, from Calvi. He spent his early childhood in Moriani-Plage in Haute-Corse before the family moved to Marseille. He earned a vocational qualification in food service and a professional baccalauréat in hospitality, then trained for six months as a mechanical fitter in aeronautics, landing a job at the helicopter manufacturer Eurocopter.

Discovery and Modeling Career

In January 2007, a talent scout spotted Giabiconi at a Marseille sports club and suggested he approach a local modeling agency run by a photographer. In 2008, he signed with DNA Model Management in New York, the same year Karl Lagerfeld named him the male face of Chanel — a role in which he closed the house's couture shows in 2009 with Freja Beha Erichsen and Lara Stone, and again in 2010.

The Karl Lagerfeld Years

Giabiconi became widely known as Lagerfeld's muse starting in 2008, when he was 18. He appeared in campaigns for Chanel, Fendi, and Lagerfeld's own eponymous label, largely shot by Lagerfeld himself, as well as in the designer's short films The Tale of a Fairy (2011) and Once Upon a Time... (2013). He also became the face of Coca-Cola Light alongside Coco Rocha, in a campaign photographed by Lagerfeld, and appeared in a 2010 Roberto Cavalli campaign alongside Kate Moss.

In his 2020 book Karl et moi, written with Jean-François Kervéan, Giabiconi described his relationship with Lagerfeld as close and paternal rather than romantic, saying Lagerfeld had considered adopting him and that the two exchanged deep affection despite the age gap. Lagerfeld's cat, Choupette, was originally Giabiconi's before he gave her to the designer, who grew attached to her.

Models.com

Giabiconi was ranked number one on Models.com's list of the Top 50 international male models for roughly two years before being succeeded by Sean O'Pry, and remains on the site's "Icons" list.

Music, Television, and Business

Giabiconi released his debut single, "Showtime," in 2010, and his debut album, Oxygen, in September 2012, which entered the French albums chart at number one. In late 2011, he competed on the French edition of Dancing with the Stars, Danse avec les stars, finishing third. He has also opened restaurants under the name Tokio Sushi, launched a 2015 streetwear capsule collection with Eleven Paris featuring photographs by Lagerfeld, and published a 2016 photo calendar shot by Mariano Vivanco. In 2017, he briefly served as president of French football club FC Martigues.

Personal Life

Giabiconi became a father on July 17, 2023, announcing the birth of his son on Instagram.

Editorial Note

Baptiste Giabiconi's career continues to be documented through independent editorial archives dedicated to preserving the history of commercial male modeling.

His standing as Karl Lagerfeld's muse from 2008 onward, his years as the male face of Chanel and Fendi, his roughly two-year run at #1 on Models.com's Top 50, and his crossover into music, television, and business collectively establish his place among the defining figures of 21st-century male modeling.
