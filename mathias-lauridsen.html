Early Life

Mathias Lauridsen was born on January 13, 1984, in Copenhagen, Denmark. He began modeling at age 19, signing with Copenhagen's Scoop Models as his mother agency, with early jobs for Shoe Magazine and Another Magazine.

Discovery and Modeling Career

Lauridsen made his runway debut in the Spring 2004 shows, walking for Marc by Marc Jacobs in New York and Miu Miu in Paris. His breakthrough came the same year when he became the face of Dior Homme's Spring/Summer campaign, photographed by Inez van Lamsweerde and Vinoodh Matadin, and he was also photographed by Richard Avedon for a Hermès Fall/Winter campaign — one of the celebrated photographer's final projects before his death later that year.

Lauridsen went on to model for Prada, Jil Sander, Gucci, Hugo Boss, Givenchy, Louis Vuitton, Christian Dior, Kenzo, Calvin Klein, Marc Jacobs, DKNY, Tommy Hilfiger, Lacoste, Fendi, Bottega Veneta, Carolina Herrera, Emporio Armani, Coach, Cartier, Burberry, H&M, Salvatore Ferragamo, Diesel, Missoni, and Massimo Dutti, and became the face of the Gucci Pour Homme II fragrance. His runway credentials include opening shows for Hermès, Belstaff, and Jill Stuart, and closing for Missoni, Gucci, Valentino, and John Galliano. He has worked with photographers including Mario Testino, Steven Meisel, Karl Lagerfeld, David Sims, Patrick Demarchelier, and Craig McDean, and appeared in Vogue Italia, L'Uomo Vogue, Vogue Paris, Numéro Homme, and international editions of GQ. He has continued modeling into recent years, including runway and campaign work for Prada, Louis Vuitton, and Gucci through 2023–2025.

Recognition

THE 100 / MaleIconic

Lauridsen is featured in THE 100: The Definitive Record of the Greatest Male Models in History and profiled on MaleIconic.com's companion archive.

Vogue, Models.com & Forbes

In 2014, Vogue named Lauridsen one of the "Top 10 Male Models of All Time." He was ranked number one on Models.com's "Top 50 Male Models" list for more than two years, the youngest model to reach that position, and was ranked second on Forbes' list of the world's most successful male models. In July 2014, Models.com also included him among its "Top Icons Men" and "The Money Guys."

Personal Life

Lauridsen married Swedish stylist Zara Lou Zachrisson on August 30, 2014, in New York; Zachrisson's sister, singer Lykke Li, performed at their wedding reception. The couple has two sons. Known in the industry as the "Danish Prince," Lauridsen is distinguished by a scar on his left cheek and is signed with Ford Models in Paris, IMG in New York, and Scoop in Copenhagen.
