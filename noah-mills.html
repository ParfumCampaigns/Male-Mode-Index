Early Life

Noah Mills was born on April 26, 1983, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, and raised in Baltimore, Maryland, before attending boarding schools in Canada and Australia. He is the youngest of five children, with two older brothers and two older sisters. After boarding school, he planned to study psychology in college.

Discovery and Modeling Career

While attending university in Vancouver in 2003, Mills was approached by a local scout who asked if he'd be interested in modeling. He soon relocated to New York and, within weeks, booked his first runway show for Gucci. He signed with Wilhelmina Models and went on to walk for Gucci, Yves Saint Laurent, Versace, Michael Kors, and Dolce & Gabbana, making his runway debut for the latter two houses' fall collections in Milan and Paris in 2004.

In early 2005, Mills signed with Dolce & Gabbana, becoming one of the defining faces of the brand through the mid-2000s and remaining a fixture of its shows, alongside Versace and Michael Kors, for years afterward — for Michael Kors, he opened alongside Carmen Kass beginning in spring 2008. In 2009, he appeared in the Dolce & Gabbana Anthology Fragrance campaign alongside Claudia Schiffer, Naomi Campbell, and Tyson Ballou. He also modeled for Salvatore Ferragamo, Tommy Hilfiger, and Tom Ford, working with photographers including Steven Meisel, Peter Lindbergh, Steven Klein, Mario Testino, Mikael Jansson, Craig McDean, and Mert & Marcus.

Vogue

In September 2014, Vogue named Mills one of the "Top 10 Male Models of All Time." Models.com has described him as one of the industry's most sought-after names.

Male Model Index

Noah Mills is recognized on the Male Model Index as one of the defining faces of Dolce & Gabbana's 2000s advertising and a rare successful model-to-actor crossover.

Acting Career

Mills made his film debut with a short, The Best Man, in 2010, and the same year had a starring role opposite Kim Cattrall's Samantha Jones in Sex and the City 2. From 2011 to 2012, he had a recurring role as Robbie on CBS's 2 Broke Girls, and appeared in Taylor Swift's 2012 music video for "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together" as her former love interest. He wrote, co-produced, and starred in the 2013 short film WRACKED, for which he won Best Actor at the Golden Egg Film Festival.

Mills went on to co-star in NBC's The Brave (2017–18) and The Enemy Within (2019), appeared in Disney+'s The Falcon and the Winter Soldier (2020), and starred as Special Agent Jesse Boone in CBS's NCIS: Hawaiʻi from 2021 to 2024. In 2025, he joined the CBS series Watson in a recurring role.

Editorial Note

Noah Mills's career continues to be documented through independent editorial archives dedicated to preserving the history of commercial male modeling.
