Early Life and Athletic Career

Tomás Valdemar Hintnaus was born on February 15, 1958, in Brazil to Czech immigrant parents, Lubomir and Marianne Hintnaus, who had fled Czechoslovakia for West Germany before an intermediate stop in Brazil and an eventual move to the United States in 1960. He attended Aviation High School in Redondo Beach, California, and won the CIF California State Meet in pole vault in 1976.

Hintnaus won the pole vault at the 1980 United States Olympic Trials with a jump of 5.60 meters (18 feet 4½ inches), but the United States' boycott of the 1980 Moscow Summer Olympics kept him from competing there. He later competed for his native Brazil in Olympic competition.

Discovery and Modeling Career

At a track meet in 1979, Hintnaus was noticed by a photographer for Track and Field News, who encouraged him to try modeling. In 1982, he became the first model in Calvin Klein's underwear advertising, photographed by Bruce Weber leaning against a white-washed chimney on the Greek island of Santorini. The image ran on a Times Square billboard and at New York bus stops, where copies were reportedly stolen by admirers, and became one of the defining images of male-focused advertising in the 1980s.

Hintnaus later reflected on the disproportion between his athletic ambitions and his public image, saying he ended up being more well known for putting on a pair of briefs than for his pole-vaulting career. He also posed for a widely circulated 1984 pin-up poster.

Recognition

THE 100 / MaleIconic

Hintnaus is featured in THE 100: The Definitive Record of the Greatest Male Models in History and profiled on MaleIconic.com's companion archive.

American Photographer

The Bruce Weber photograph of Hintnaus was named one of "10 Pictures That Changed America" by American Photographer magazine, and has since been reproduced on the cover of a Calvin Klein retrospective book featuring work by Weber, Irving Penn, Richard Avedon, and Patrick Demarchelier.

Male Model Index

Hintnaus is not listed among the 25 names on L'Uomo Vogue's 2012 "Top 25 Male Models Ever" list.

Acting and Later Life

Hintnaus appeared in a season-four episode of Hawaii Five-0 as an assassin, among other television and film credits. He has lived in Honolulu, Hawaii, where he has worked building decks for private homes, and has continued to compete in pole vault into later life.
