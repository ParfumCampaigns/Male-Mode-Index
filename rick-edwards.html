Early Life and Athletic Career

Rick Edwards was born in Northfield, Massachusetts. He attended Amherst College, where he rowed with the oar team, and went on to row professionally for four years with the New York Athletic Club, competing at the 1978 World Rowing Championships.

Modeling Career

Edwards's rowing brought him to the attention of a modeling agent, launching a modeling career built largely on his athletic build. He became a favorite model of the Ford agency and a regular presence on the cover of Gentlemen's Quarterly (GQ) in the early 1980s, including a December 1981 cover photographed by Rico Puhlmann, alongside advertising work for clients including Christian Dior. He also briefly took up boxing during this period, a pursuit he later said he regretted having to give up after two years, since it was considered ill-advised for a working model.

Acting Career

Looking for a change of career, Edwards moved into supporting film roles, appearing in Skatetown, U.S.A. (1979) and the TV movie High Performance (1983). His first major acting role came the same year in the Italian medieval drama I Paladini — storia d'armi e d'amori, playing the knight Orlando. In 1987, he joined the daytime drama Santa Barbara as Jake Morton. He also has stunt and script/continuity credits, including work on Predator 2.

Personal Life

Edwards has three children, two daughters and a son.
